Permit for medical treatment after entry visa

Documents for first issuance Documents for renewal

ğŸ“ What is it?

The residence permit for medical treatment allows you to stay in Italy to undergo specialist medical examinations and receive necessary treatment.

â“ Who can apply for it?

Those who have a visa for "medical treatment" purposes, meaning a visa that authorizes entry into Italy and staying for a limited period to receive treatment at an Italian healthcare facility, or those who enter Italy as part of humanitarian programs.

â±ï¸ How long does it last?

The duration of the medical treatment permit depends on the expected duration of the treatment you will undergo in Italy.

ğŸ’¼ What does it allow me to do?

Work: the residence permit for "medical treatment" authorizes you to work in Italy. This is expressly provided for in article 36, comma 3 of D.Lgs. n. 286/1998. However, upon expiration, you cannot request conversion to a work permit.

ğŸ¥ Can I register with the National Health Service?

No. If you entered with a visa, you are not entitled to register with the health system. This means that the cost of medical examinations and treatments you receive in Italy is entirely at your expense.

ğŸ“‹ How do I request the visa?

You must contact the Italian embassy or consulate in your country of origin.

Documents required for the visa:

  1. Valid passport (expiration at least 3 months after the visa)
  2. Medical certificate about the condition, issued by an Italian or foreign healthcare facility (translated into Italian)
  3. Declaration from the Italian healthcare facility where you will be treated (type of treatment, start date, expected duration)
  4. Payment to the healthcare facility of an amount equal to 30% of the estimated cost of treatment
  5. Proof of sufficient financial resources for treatment, food, accommodation and travel

You must go within 8 days to the Police Headquarters (Questura) of the place where you have your domicile to request the issuance of the residence permit for "medical treatment". Your accompanying person, if any, must do the same.

Upon expiration, the "medical treatment" permit can be renewed: the requirements are the same as for the first issuance.
