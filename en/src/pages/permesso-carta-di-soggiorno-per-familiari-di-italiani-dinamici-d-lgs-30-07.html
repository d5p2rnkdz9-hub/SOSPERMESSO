Permit for Residence card for family members of "dynamic" Italians (D.Lgs. 30/07)

Documents for first issuance Documents for renewal

What is this residence card?

It is a residence permit given to non-EU family members of a "dynamic" Italian citizen (i.e. one who has lived stably in another European Union country, typically for work, and who now lives in Italy).

What is this card?

It is a residence permit given to non-EU family members of a "dynamic" Italian citizen. A "dynamic" Italian citizen is an Italian citizen who has lived or worked in another European Union country other than Italy.

How long does this card last?

It lasts 5 years and can be renewed. After the first renewal, it becomes permanent.

Who can apply for this card?

You can apply if you are:

  • Wife/husband or civil partner of the Italian citizen
  • Partner who has signed a cohabitation agreement, even without marriage or civil union
  • Son/daughter up to 21 years old of the Italian citizen or their spouse
  • Son/daughter over 21 years old of the "dynamic" Italian citizen or their spouse, but dependent (you must show that you are dependent: documents from your country of origin, money sent to you from abroad, some Police Headquarters also require proof that you are a tax dependent of the Italian family member)
  • Mother/father of the "dynamic" Italian citizen or their spouse, but dependent (you must show that you are dependent: documents from your country of origin, money sent to you from abroad, some Police Headquarters also require proof that you are a tax dependent of the Italian family member)
  • Grandmother/grandfather of the "dynamic" Italian citizen or their spouse
  • Grandchild of the "dynamic" Italian citizen or their spouse up to 21 years old
  • Grandchild of the "dynamic" Italian citizen or their spouse, over 21 years old and dependent (you must show that you are dependent: documents from your country of origin, money sent to you from abroad, some Police Headquarters also require proof that you are a tax dependent of the Italian family member)

Where do I apply for this card?

You must submit the application to the Police Headquarters (Questura) of the area where you live.

You must apply within 3 months of entering Italy. If you are already in Italy with another type of visa (tourism, family, etc.), you can change it to a residence card if you have all the requirements.

What documents do I need to apply?

  • Valid passport (page with your personal data and expiry, any visas and entry/exit stamps)
  • Your old residence permit (if you have one)
  • Identity document of the Italian citizen
  • Declaration of hospitality
  • Family status certificate of the Italian citizen (given by the municipality where they live)
  • Documents showing the degree of relationship (if from abroad must be translated and legalized/apostilled)
  • Documents showing the Italian citizen's income (CU/730/Unico) (when required that the non-EU family member is dependent on the dynamic Italian family member)
  • Documents showing that you are dependent (when required that the non-EU family member is dependent on the dynamic Italian family member)
  • Documents showing that the Italian citizen has lived in another EU country (according to art. 23 D.Lgs. 30 of 2007)

What can I do with this residence card?

With this card you can:

  • Work with a regular contract (self-employed or employed)
  • Enroll in school, vocational training, or university
  • Register with the National Health Service (SSN) and have a family doctor
  • Travel in Schengen countries for up to 90 days every 6 months, if you have a valid passport
  • After 5 years you can apply for the permanent Residence card
