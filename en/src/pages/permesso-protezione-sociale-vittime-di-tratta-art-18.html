Permit for Social protection for trafficking victims (art. 18)

Documents for first issuance Documents for renewal

What it is and how to apply

The residence permit for social protection reasons concerns foreign citizens subject to a situation of violence or victims of prostitution exploitation, labor exploitation and against whom concrete dangers may arise for their safety.

The special protection permit is requested by:

  • the Public Prosecutor in cases where criminal proceedings have been initiated regarding acts of violence or serious exploitation against the foreigner.
  • the social services of local authorities;
  • associations, bodies and other organizations registered in the appropriate register at the Ministry of Labor and Social Policies and authorized to implement assistance and social protection programs for foreigners.

The Police Headquarters (Questura), upon receiving the request and verifying the existence of the required conditions, proceeds to issue the residence permit for humanitarian reasons.

What are the requirements to obtain this permit?

  • You must be a victim of violence or serious exploitation.
  • You must be in danger for having tried to escape from exploiters or for having testified against them.
  • You must participate in a social assistance and integration program.
  • If you decide to return to your country, if there is risk for your safety and that of your family members, this must be considered.

Is it necessary to file a complaint to obtain the residence permit?

No. The permit under art. 18 can in fact be issued in two ways:

  • Judicial path: the victim reports the exploiters and the permit is granted with the opinion of the Prosecutor.
  • Social path: the victim does not report, but enters an assistance program managed by specialized bodies.

What documents and requirements are needed?

  • opinion of the Public Prosecutor, in cases where the Prosecutor gives indications about the gravity and current nature of the danger;
  • participation of the foreigner in a social assistance and integration program prepared by local authorities or by private entities registered in the appropriate register established at the Ministry of Labor and affiliated with the local authority.
  • acceptance of the commitments related to the program by the person responsible for the facility where the program is to be carried out.

How long does it last?

The permit lasts 6 months, renewable for 1 year or for the longer period required for reasons of justice. It can be revoked in case of interruption of the social assistance and integration program or conduct incompatible with the purposes of the program, reported by the Public Prosecutor or, as far as relevant, by the social service of the local authority, or in any case verified by the Police Commissioner, or when the other conditions that justified its issuance no longer exist.

Can I convert it?

The permit is convertible into a residence permit for study or work reasons.
