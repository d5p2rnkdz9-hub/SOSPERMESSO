Permit for Medical treatment for pregnant woman and father of the child (art. 19)

Documents for first issuance Documents for renewal

What is this permit?

The residence permit for medical treatment/pregnancy is given to foreign women who are expecting a baby or who have just given birth

This permit protects the mother and the baby ğŸ¤±, because pregnant women or women with small children can never be sent away from Italy.

Who can apply?

For the mother:

  • During pregnancy: until the birth of the baby
  • After childbirth: for 6 months after birth

For the father:

  • If married to the mother: also during pregnancy
  • If not married: only after the birth of the baby and after having recognized the child
  • For 6 months after the birth of the baby

How long does this permit last?

  • During pregnancy: until the birth of the baby
  • After birth: 6 months

Where do you apply for this permit?

Go in person to the Police Headquarters (Questura) of the city where you live to make an appointment.

For the pregnant mother:

  • Medical certificate showing that you are pregnant and when the baby will be born (from a public hospital or private doctor with ASL stamp) ğŸ¥
  • Declaration of hospitality (where you live)
  • 4 photos
  • Revenue stamp of 16 euros
  • If you have one, a valid passport (but they should give you the permit even if you don't have a passport)

For the married father:

  • The same documents as the mother plus
  • Marriage certificate translated and legalized (or with apostille)

For the unmarried father:

  • The same documents as the mother plus
  • Paternity certificate after recognition of the child

ğŸ“‹ What documents are needed for renewal (after birth)?

  • Birth certificate of the baby with names of mother and father
  • Declaration of hospitality
  • 4 photos
  • Revenue stamp of 16 euros
  • Valid passport of the mother and the baby (for the baby, the consular identity attestation is also acceptable)

With this permit you can:

  • Register with the National Health Service (SSN) and have a family doctor
  • Receive free assistance during pregnancy and childbirth

When it expires, can I convert it to another?

After 6 months from childbirth, this permit can be converted to:

  • Permit for family reasons
  • Warning! You cannot convert this permit to a work permit
