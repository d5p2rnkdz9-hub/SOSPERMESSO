Permit for FAMIT for family members of "static" Italian citizens

Documents for first issuance Documents for renewal

What is this permit?

It is a residence permit given to family members of an Italian citizen who has never lived in other European Union countries.

How long does this permit last?

It lasts 5 years.

Who can apply?

The following can apply:

  • Wife/husband of the Italian citizen (or civil union partner)
  • Partners who have signed a cohabitation contract, even without marriage or civil union
  • Children up to 21 years old (of the Italian citizen or spouse)
  • Children over 21 years old (of the Italian citizen or spouse) dependent on the family. For this you need to provide proof (documents from the country of origin, receipts of money sent, and sometimes being registered as fiscally dependent)
  • Parents (of the Italian citizen or spouse) dependent on the family. For this you need to provide proof (documents from the country of origin, receipts of money sent, and sometimes being registered as fiscally dependent)
  • Grandparents (of the Italian citizen or spouse)
  • Grandchildren (children of children) up to 21 years old (of the Italian citizen or spouse)
  • Grandchildren (children of children) over 21 years old (of the Italian citizen or spouse) dependent on the family. For this you need to provide proof (documents from the country of origin, receipts of money sent, and sometimes being registered as fiscally dependent)

Warning: Brothers, sisters, uncles, cousins and other relatives are not entitled to this permit. For them, only a "facilitation" for entry and stay in Italy is provided.

What documents do I need?

At the time of the application you will need to bring:

  • Valid passport or photo document attesting your nationality
  • Expiring/expired residence permit if you have one
  • Identity document of the Italian family member
  • Documentation showing your legal presence in Italy
  • Documentation showing that your family has sufficient income relative to the number of people in it (this income must come from legal sources and must not be less than the annual amount required by law for each family member)
  • Family certificate and residence certificate of the Italian family member (if done with self-certification, also include copies of identity documents of each person in the family)
  • Registered rental contract or registered deed of ownership, in the name of the Italian family member
  • Health insurance for at least 12 months for non-EU family members over 65 (except for parents of Italian citizens)
  • Documentation proving the family relationship
  • Proof that you are "dependent" on the Italian family member, such as certificates from your country of origin valid for 180 days. These documents must be translated and, if necessary, authenticated with apostille or by a notary
  • Declaration from the Italian family member guaranteeing your support and confirming that you live together
  • Declaration of hospitality

What does this allow me to do?

With this permit you can:

  • Have a 5-year residence permit
  • Register your residence where you live
  • Work with a regular contract (self-employment or employed work)
  • Register with the National Health Service (SSN) and have a family doctor
  • Travel freely in Italy and in Schengen countries (with a valid passport)
  • Access training courses, schools and universities

How can I renew it?

Before expiration, you can renew this permit at the Police Headquarters (Questura) in your area, presenting:

  • Valid passport
  • Family certificate and residence certificate
  • Identity document of the Italian family member
  • Any other documents that may be requested

Can I convert it?

If you meet the requirements, this permit can be converted to:

  • Work permit
  • Study permit
