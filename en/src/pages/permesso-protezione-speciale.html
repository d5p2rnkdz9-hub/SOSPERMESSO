Residence permit for special protection

Documents for first issuance Documents for renewal

What is this permit?

It is a residence permit that can be given to those who are well-established in Italy, who are very vulnerable, or who would face particular dangers in their country. This permit protects foreigners who cannot be sent away from Italy because in their country they could suffer persecution, torture or inhuman treatment.

How long does this permit last?

It has a validity of two years.

How can I get it?

The residence permit for special protection can be given:

  • By the Territorial Commission after you have applied for international protection ("asylum" application)
  • By a court after a negative decision from the Commission (usually Courts grant it more easily than Commissions). In this case you definitely already have a lawyer and we recommend you ask them for advice.

Where do you apply for this permit?

If the Territorial Commission or the Court decides you can have special protection:

  • You must go to the Police Headquarters (Questura) to apply for the permit
  • They will give you an appointment for fingerprinting
  • The Police Headquarters (Questura) will tell you when the permit is ready

Can I apply directly at the Police Headquarters (Questura), without applying for asylum?

Since 2023 the law has changed and it has become much more difficult to get a permit for special protection directly from the Police Headquarters (Questura). There are Court rulings that say you can continue to apply directly to the Police Headquarters (Questura), but we recommend you seek help from a good lawyer.

Additional specific documents:

  • Decree from the Territorial Commission recognizing special protection (or court decision)
  • Residence certificate or documents showing where you live
  • Any supporting medical or psychological documentation
  • Documentation demonstrating roots in Italy (if applicable)

What can I do with this residence permit?

With this permit you can:

  • Work with a regular contract (self-employed or employed)
  • Get residence in the Municipality where you live
  • Enroll in the National Health Service (SSN) and have a general practitioner
  • Travel in the Schengen area with passport (for stays up to 3 months)
  • Enroll in school, vocational course, or university

  • If you applied for asylum or for a special protection permit BEFORE May 6, 2023: you can convert it to a work permit (self-employed or employed), if you have all the requirements.
  • If you applied for asylum or for a special protection permit AFTER May 6, 2023: you CANNOT convert your permit to a work permit

Warning: To avoid mistakes, first seek help from a free legal assistance center.

Can I renew the special protection permit?

We cannot give you a certain answer at the moment.

  • If you got the special protection permit because you applied BEFORE May 6, 2023, we recommend always requesting conversion to another permit, if possible. Only ask for renewal if you have no other choice. It will be renewed for one year, and then it is unclear what will happen.
  • If you got the special protection permit because you applied AFTER May 6, 2023: you can try to convert your permit to a work permit
