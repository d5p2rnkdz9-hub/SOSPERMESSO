Cohabitants with Italian family members within the second degree

Documents for first issuance Documents for renewal

What is this?

The residence permit for cohabitants with Italian citizens is given to foreigners who live in Italy together with Italian family members within the second degree (wife/husband; mother/father; son/daughter; grandmother/grandfather; grandchild; brother/sister).

How long does it last?

It has a validity of two years.

What are the requirements to obtain it?

The two fundamental elements for this permit are proof of family relationship and living together.

Family relationship

You are entitled to this permit if you are:

  • brother/sister of the Italian citizen
  • spouse (or civil partner, for same-sex persons) of the Italian citizen
  • parent of the Italian citizen
  • son/daughter (of any age) of the Italian citizen
  • grandfather/grandmother of the Italian citizen
  • grandchild of the Italian citizen

Living together

It is necessary that you live with your Italian relative. You must be registered at the same address, even if you don't live together 100% of the time.

Where do I apply for this permit?

You must submit the application to the Police Headquarters (Questura) of the area where you live with the Italian citizen, attaching all required documents. You will be given a receipt that will allow you to stay in Italy legally until the decision.

During the evaluation phase, the Police Headquarters (Questura) may ask you for additional documents and possibly conduct interviews to verify your actual cohabitation with the Italian citizen.

What can I do with this permit?

With this permit you can:

  • Get residence in the Municipality where you live
  • Work (self-employed or employed)
  • Register with the National Health Service (SSN) and have a family doctor
  • Travel in the Schengen area, if you have a valid passport
  • Enroll in training courses, schools and universities

What happens when the permit expires?

Before it expires, you can:

  • Renew it
  • Convert it to another permit, such as work (self-employed or employed) or study
