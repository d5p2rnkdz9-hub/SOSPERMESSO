Serious health reasons

Documents for first issuance Documents for renewal

ğŸ“ What is it?

The residence permit for medical treatment is given to foreigners who have very serious illnesses and that cannot be treated properly in their country.

If these people returned to their country, their health would get much worse or they could even die.

â“ Who can apply?

You can apply for this permit if:

  • You have a very serious illness that cannot be treated properly in your country
  • You are already in Italy (even without a residence permit)
  • An Italian doctor (from a public hospital or one affiliated with the National Health Service) has confirmed that your illness is serious
  • If you returned to your country, your health would get much worse

ğŸ“ Where do you apply for this permit?

Go to the Police Headquarters (Questura) in the area where you live and book an appointment at the Immigration Office.

The application is made with form 209 (not with the postal kit).

IT'S FREE! For this permit you DON'T pay:

  • The 30.46 euros for the electronic permit
  • The residence contribution

â±ï¸ How long does it last?

  • Maximum 1 year (can be less, depends on what the doctor says)
  • Can be renewed as long as you still have the serious illness and need treatment

âœ… What can you do with this permit?

With this permit you can:

  • Work (with a regular contract - self-employment or employed work)
  • Register with the National Health Service (SSN) and have a family doctor
  • Receive free medical treatment in public hospitals
  • Register with the registry office of the Municipality where you live
  • Sign contracts for rent and insurance

To renew you must show:

  • New medical certificate confirming that you still have the serious illness
  • Hospital declaration stating that you must continue treatment in Italy
  • The same documents as the first application

This permit can be converted to:

  • Work permit (if you find a contract and meet all requirements)
  • Family reasons permit (if you have Italian or European family)

If you are too ill, the application can be made by:

  • A family member (with power of attorney)
  • The head of the hospital where you are hospitalized
  • Anyone who has an interest in helping you
