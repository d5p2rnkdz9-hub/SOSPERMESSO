Permit for Employed work

When can I have a work permit?

You can obtain a residence permit for employed work only in two cases:

  1. You entered Italy with a work visa
    For example: visa for "annual immigration quota decree", seasonal work visa, or other specific visa for work reasons
  2. You already have another residence permit that can be converted
    Some permits (such as study, family, and others) can be converted to a work permit if you have certain requirements

Warning: in Italy it is NOT possible to arrive without a visa (or with a tourist visa) and then apply for a work residence permit.

Even if you already have a job offer, you cannot directly obtain a work permit if you don't fall into one of the cases above.

I have a job offer, can I get the permit?

No, having a job offer is not enough.

If you are already in Italy with a residence permit, check if your permit can be converted to a work permit.

If you don't have a residence permit, your future employer could apply for you during the next "annual immigration quota decree" - but you will still have to return to your country to get the visa and then re-enter Italy.
