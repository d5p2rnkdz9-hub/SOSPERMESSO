FAMIT permit for family members of static Italian citizens

Documents for first issuance Documents for renewal

What is this permit?

What is this permit? It is a residence permit given to family members of an Italian citizen who has never lived in other European Union countries.

How long does this permit last?

How long does this permit last? It lasts 5 years.

Who can apply?

Who can apply? Can apply:

  • Wife/husband of the Italian citizen (or civil partner)
  • Partners who have signed a cohabitation contract, even without marriage or civil union
  • Children up to 21 years old (of the Italian citizen or their spouse)
  • Children over 21 years old (of the Italian citizen or their spouse) who are dependents of the family. For this you need to present proof (documents from the country of origin, receipts of money sent, and sometimes being registered as a fiscal dependent)
  • Parents (of the Italian citizen or their spouse) who are dependents of the family. For this you need to present proof (documents from the country of origin, receipts of money sent, and sometimes being registered as a fiscal dependent)
  • Grandparents (of the Italian citizen or their spouse)
  • Grandchildren (children of children) up to 21 years old (of the Italian citizen or their spouse)
  • Grandchildren (children of children) over 21 years old (of the Italian citizen or their spouse) who are dependents of the family. For this you need to present proof (documents from the country of origin, receipts of money sent, and sometimes being registered as a fiscal dependent)

Attention: Brothers, sisters, uncles, cousins and other relatives are not entitled to this permit. For them only a "facilitation" for entry and stay in Italy is provided.

What documents do I need?

What documents do I need? At the time of the request you will need to bring:

  • Valid passport or document with photo proving your nationality
  • Expiring/expired residence permit if you have one
  • Identity document of the Italian family member
  • Documentation showing your regular presence in Italy
  • Documentation showing that your family has sufficient income relative to the people in it (this income must come from legal sources and must not be less than the annual amount provided by law for each family member)
  • Family and residence certificate of the Italian family member (if done by self-certification, also include copies of identity documents of each person in the family)
  • Registered rental contract or registered property deed, in the name of the Italian family member
  • Health insurance for at least 12 months for non-EU family members over 65 years old (except for parents of Italian citizens)
  • Documentation proving the family relationship
  • Proof that you are "dependent" on the Italian family member, such as certificates from your country of origin valid for 180 days. These documents must be translated and, if necessary, authenticated with apostille or by a notary
  • Declaration from the Italian family member guaranteeing your maintenance and confirming that you live together
  • Hospitality declaration

What does it allow me to do?

What does it allow me to do? With this permit you can:

  • Have a residence permit for 5 years
  • Get residence where you live
  • Work with a regular contract (self-employment or employed work)
  • Register with the National Health Service (SSN) and have a general practitioner
  • Travel freely in Italy and Schengen countries (with valid passport)
  • Access training courses, schools and universities

How can I renew it?

How can I renew it? Before expiration, you can renew this permit at the Police Headquarters (Questura) of your area, presenting:

  • Valid passport
  • Family and residence certificate
  • Identity document of the Italian family member
  • Any other documents that may be requested

Can I convert it?

Can I convert it? If you have the requirements, this permit can be converted to:

  • Work permit
  • Study permit
