Residence card for family member of "dynamic" Italian citizen

For family members of Italians who have lived in other EU countries (D.Lgs. 30/07)

ğŸ“ What is it?

It is a residence permit given to non-EU family members of a "dynamic" Italian citizen. A "dynamic" Italian citizen is an Italian citizen who has lived or worked in another European Union country other than Italy.

â±ï¸ How long does it last?

It lasts 5 years and can be renewed. After the first renewal, it becomes permanent.

â“ Who can apply for this card?

You can apply if you are:

  • Spouse or civil union partner of the Italian citizen
  • Partner who has signed a cohabitation contract, even without marriage or civil union
  • Child up to 21 years old of the Italian citizen or their spouse
  • Child over 21 years old of the "dynamic" Italian citizen or their spouse, but dependent (you must show you are dependent: documents from your country of origin, money sent to you from abroad; some Police Headquarters (Questura) also require proof that you are a tax dependent of the Italian family member)
  • Parent of the "dynamic" Italian citizen or their spouse, but dependent
  • Grandparent of the "dynamic" Italian citizen or their spouse
  • Grandchild up to 21 years old of the "dynamic" Italian citizen or their spouse
  • Grandchild over 21 years old of the "dynamic" Italian citizen or their spouse, and dependent

ğŸ“ Where do I apply for this card?

You must submit the application to the Police Headquarters (Questura) in the area where you live.

You must apply within 3 months of entering Italy. If you are already in Italy with another type of visa (tourism, family, etc.), you can change it to a residence card if you meet all the requirements.

ğŸ“„ What documents do I need?

  • Valid passport (page with your personal data and expiry dates, any visas and entry/exit stamps)
  • Your previous residence permit (if you have one)
  • Identity document of the Italian citizen
  • Hospitality declaration
  • Family status certificate of the Italian citizen (issued by the municipality where they live)
  • Documents showing the degree of relationship (if from abroad, they must be translated and legalized/apostilled)
  • Documents showing income of the Italian citizen (CU/730/Unico) (when the non-EU family member is required to be dependent on the "dynamic" Italian family member)
  • Documents showing you are dependent (when the non-EU family member is required to be dependent on the "dynamic" Italian family member)
  • Documents showing the Italian citizen has lived in another EU country (according to art. 23 D.lvo 30 of 2007)

ğŸ’¼ What can I do with this card?

With this card you can:

  • Work with a regular contract (self-employment or employed work)
  • Enroll in school, a professional course, or university
  • Register with the National Health Service (SSN) and have a general practitioner
  • Travel in Schengen countries up to 90 days every 6 months, if you have a valid passport
  • After 5 years you can apply for a Permanent residence card
