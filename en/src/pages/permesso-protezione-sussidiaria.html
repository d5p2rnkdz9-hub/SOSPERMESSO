Permit for Subsidiary protection

Documents for first issuance Documents for renewal

What is this permit?

It is the permit given to those who flee their country because there is war or the risk of being killed, sentenced to death, tortured or suffering other serious mistreatment.

How do I apply for the permit?

To get this permit you must:

  • Apply for asylum (or international protection, it's the same thing - see our guide)
  • Obtain a positive decision from the Territorial Commission or the Court
  • With this positive decision, go to the Police Headquarters (Questura) to apply for the permit

How long does it last?

The permit lasts 5 years and can be renewed, usually automatically, for another 5 years.

What can I do with this permit?

With this permit you can:

  • Get residence in your municipality
  • Work legally (employed or self-employed)
  • Enroll in the National Health Service (SSN) and have a general practitioner
  • Enroll in school, vocational course, or university
  • Receive financial and housing assistance
  • Apply for family reunification with close family members:
    • wife/husband
    • minor children
    • adult children if totally disabled
    • parents over 65 years old or financially dependent

For family reunification you do NOT need to prove that you have a large house and a minimum salary like other foreigners.

Can I travel outside Italy?

Travel freely in Italy, go to other EU countries for up to 3 months.

If you cannot get a passport from your country, you can try to apply for the "travel document" (a kind of Italian passport that allows you to travel to other countries as well).

WARNING! You cannot travel to your country of origin. If you do, the Police Headquarters (Questura) could revoke your residence permit.

Where do you apply for this permit?

You must go in person to the Police Headquarters (Questura).

What documents do I need to apply?

To apply for the permit you must bring the decision from the Commission (or the Court) that granted you subsidiary protection.

Can I renew it when it expires?

Yes, usually the subsidiary protection permit is renewed for another five years. But be careful! If the conditions of danger in your country no longer exist, the permit might not be renewed.

Can I convert this permit?

Before it expires, you can decide not to renew the subsidiary protection permit but to request conversion to a residence permit for self-employed or employed work if you work legally, to a study permit if you are enrolled in a training course or university, or to an EU residence permit for long-term residents ("Card"), after five years of residence in Italy.
