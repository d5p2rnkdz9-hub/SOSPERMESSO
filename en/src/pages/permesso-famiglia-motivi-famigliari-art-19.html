Permit for Family - family reasons (art. 19)

Documents for first issuance Documents for renewal

What is this permit?

The residence permit for cohabitants with Italian citizens is given to foreigners who live in Italy together with Italian family members within the second degree (husband/wife; mother/father; son/daughter; grandmother/grandfather; grandchild; brother/sister).

How long does this permit last?

It is valid for two years.

What are the conditions to obtain it?

The two fundamental elements for this permit are proof of the family relationship and living together.

Family relationship: You are entitled to this permit if you are

  • brother/sister of the Italian citizen
  • spouse (or civil partner, for same-sex persons) of the Italian citizen
  • parent of the Italian citizen
  • son/daughter (of any age) of the Italian citizen
  • grandfather/grandmother of the Italian citizen
  • grandchild (child of children) of the Italian citizen

Living together: it is necessary that you live with your Italian relative. You must be registered at the same address, even if you don't live together 100% of the time.

Where do you apply for this permit?

You must submit the application to the Police Headquarters (Questura) of the territory where you cohabit with the Italian citizen, attaching all required documents. You will be given a receipt that will allow you to remain legally in Italy until the decision.

During the evaluation phase, the Police Headquarters (Questura) may ask you to supplement the documents you have provided and possibly conduct interviews to verify your actual cohabitation with the Italian citizen.

What documents do I need to apply?

(https://questure.poliziadistato.it/it/Venezia/articolo/214963dcfc880e387930055883)

  • Proof of certain residence: updated family status certificate or hospitality declaration validated by the Public Safety Authority or lease contract co-signed and registered with the Revenue Agency.
  • Certificates proving the family relationship (translated and legalized or apostilled)
    • For spouse: marriage certificate
    • For children: birth certificate with paternity and maternity
    • For cohesion with another relative within the 2nd degree: birth or family certificates showing that the Italian citizen is your relative
  • Copy of the Italian relative's identity document
  • Proof of cohabitation (updated family status certificate of the Italian citizen and hospitality declaration validated by the Public Safety Authority for the foreigner or lease contract co-signed by both and registered with the Revenue Agency)
  • If you have them: old residence permit and tax code
  • Revenue stamp of 16.00 â‚¬
  • Valid passport
  • 4 passport photos of the applicant, plus 4 passport photos of any minor children under 14 to be included in the residence permit (children must be present at the time of summons);

What can I do with this residence permit?

With this permit you can:

  • Get residence in the Municipality where you live
  • Work (self-employment or employed work)
  • Register with the National Health Service (SSN) and have a general practitioner
  • Travel in the Schengen area, if you have a valid passport
  • Enroll in training courses, schools and universities

And when the permit expires?

Before it expires, you can

  • renew it
  • convert it to another permit, such as work (self-employment or employed work) or study
