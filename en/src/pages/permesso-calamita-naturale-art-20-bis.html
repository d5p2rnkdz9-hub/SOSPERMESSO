Permit for Natural disaster (art. 20 bis)

Documents for first issuance Documents for renewal

What is this permit?

The residence permit for natural disaster (Art. 20bis, Consolidated Immigration Act) is given to foreigners who cannot return to their country because there is a serious emergency (such as earthquakes, wars, natural disasters) that makes living there dangerous.

This permit is different from applying for political asylum and you don't need to apply for international protection.

When can I apply for it?

You can apply for this permit if:

  • You already live in Italy
  • In your country there is a very serious and dangerous situation (emergency, calamity, disaster)
  • You cannot return to your country safely
  • The situation in your country puts your fundamental rights at risk (right to life, freedom, to decide about your life)

The situation must be exceptional and temporary but also very dangerous.

How and where do I apply for this permit?

You must submit the application to the Police Headquarters (Questura) of the area where you live.

You must go directly to the Police Headquarters (Questura) and request an appointment

After you apply, the Police Headquarters (Questura) can check if there really is a dangerous situation in your country, and then will decide.

What documents do you need?

  • Documents showing the dangerous situation in your country: newspaper articles, reports from international organizations, official documents showing that there is a serious emergency in your country
  • Copy of documents showing how you support yourself in Italy (if you have them): payslip, employment contract, benefits you receive
  • Passport or similar document
  • 2 photos
  • Revenue stamp of 16 EUR

How long does it last?

The duration of the permit is six months, renewable for another six months if

What rights does it give me? What do I have access to?

With this permit you can:

  • Get residence in the Municipality where you live
  • Work (with a regular contract - self-employed or employed)
  • Register with the National Health Service (SSN) and have a family doctor
  • Enroll in school, vocational training, or university
