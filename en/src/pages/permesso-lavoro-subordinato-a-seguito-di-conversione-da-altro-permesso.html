Permit for Employed work following conversion from another permit

Documents for first issuance Documents for renewal

What is this permit?

The residence permit for employed work is given to foreigners who work as employees of an employer with a regular contract. This means you have a boss who pays you a salary and you are their employee.

How do I apply for this permit?

The residence permit for employed work can be issued in two situations ‚ö†Ô∏è: -entering Italy with a clearance certificate (nulla osta) or a visa for employed work, within the quotas established by the annual immigration quota decree (1) -converting a previous residence permit to employed work (2)

‚úàÔ∏è 1: you entered Italy with a clearance certificate (nulla osta) and a visa for employed work:

  1. within 8 days of your arrival in Italy, go with your employer to the Single Immigration Desk for Immigration at the Prefecture of the area where you work
  2. The Single Immigration Desk will have you sign the employment contract and give you the forms to fill out
  3. Go to the post office and send the forms to the Police Headquarters (Questura)
  4. The Post Office will give you an appointment at the Police Headquarters (Questura)
  5. Go to the Police Headquarters (Questura) on the appointment day for fingerprinting
  6. The Police Headquarters (Questura) will tell you when your permit is ready

What documents do I need to apply?

  • Valid passport
  • Entry visa for employed work
  • Signed employment contract
  • Copy of your employer's identity document (if Italian or European) or copy of their residence permit (if foreign)
  • 4 recent passport photos
  • Revenue stamp of 16.00 euro
  • Payment via postal slip of 80.46 euro

üîÑ 2: you had another residence permit, then you applied for conversion to employed work permit using the postal kit

In this case, the Post Office gave you an appointment at the Police Headquarters (Questura)

  1. Go to the Police Headquarters (Questura) on the appointment day for fingerprinting
  2. If everything is in order, the Police Headquarters (Questura) will tell you when your permit is ready

What documents do I need to apply?

  • Valid passport
  • Signed employment contract
  • Copy of your employer's identity document (if Italian or European) or copy of their residence permit (if foreign)
  • 4 recent passport photos
  • Revenue stamp of 16.00 euro
  • Payment via postal slip of 80.46 euro

What are the conditions to obtain it?

To have this permit you must have a regular employment contract with an Italian employer and a payslip of at least 538 euro per month, approximately.

How long does this permit last?

The duration depends on the type of employment contract you have. Usually:

  • 2 years if your employment contract is fixed-term (has an end date)
  • 3 years if your employment contract is permanent (has no end date)

What rights does it give me? What can I access?

With this permit you can:

  • Work with a regular contract (employed or self-employed)
  • Obtain residence registration in the Municipality where you live
  • Register with the National Health Service (SSN) and have a general practitioner
  • Travel in the Schengen area with passport (for stays up to 3 months).
  • Enroll in school, vocational courses, or university
  • Apply for family reunification

What happens if I lose my job?

If you lose your job you don't lose the permit until it expires. You can:

  • Look for a new job
  • Register with unemployment lists
  • Apply for a permit for job seeking at renewal

And when the permit expires? Can I renew it? Can I convert it?

Before it expires you have two options:

  1. Renew it: renewal must be requested 60 days before the expiry date, using the postal kit.

What documents are needed?

  • Old residence permit
  • Valid passport
  • Current employment contract
  • Copy of employer's document
  • Payslips or tax return from the previous year
  • Residence certificate or hospitality declaration
  • Revenue stamp of 16 euro
  • Payment via postal slip of 80.46 euro

2. Convert it: if you have all the necessary requirements, this permit can be converted to a residence permit for:

  • Study
  • Job seeking
  • Family reasons
  • EU long-term resident card (if you have at least 5 years of residence)

What is this permit?

It is a residence permit for those who work independently, meaning they don't have an employer, but carry out a work or professional activity on their own (for example: merchant, craftsman, freelance professional, etc.).

How do I apply for this permit?

‚ÄºÔ∏è There are two different cases:

  1. ‚úàÔ∏è You entered Italy with a visa for self-employment
  2. üîÑ You want to convert another type of permit to a self-employment permit

1. ‚úàÔ∏è You entered Italy with a visa for self-employment

What you need to do:

  1. You must have already obtained a visa for self-employment from the Italian consulate in your country.
  2. Within 8 days of your arrival in Italy, fill out Form 1 and Form 2 (postal kit) and send it from an authorized post office.
  3. You will receive an appointment at the Police Headquarters (Questura) for fingerprinting.
  4. The Police Headquarters (Questura) will tell you when to collect your permit.

Documents to attach:

  • Form 1 and 2 from the kit, filled out and signed
  • Photocopy of the entire passport
  • Photocopy of the certification from the Italian diplomatic/consular representation in your country, confirming that you have the requirements for the visa for self-employment (as per art. 26 D.Lgs. 286/98 and art. 39 DPR 394/99)
  • 4 passport photos
  • Revenue stamp of 16.00 euro
  • Payment via postal slip of 80.46 euro

2. üîÑ You want to convert another type of permit to a self-employment permit

Example: you have a permit for study, family, job seeking, etc., and now you want to work as a self-employed worker.

What you need to do:

  1. Fill out the postal kit (Form 1 and 2) and send it from an authorized post office.

    Documents to attach:

  2. You will receive an appointment at the Police Headquarters (Questura) for fingerprinting.
  3. If the Police Headquarters (Questura) accepts the conversion, you will receive the new self-employment permit.

What happens if you close your business?

  • You can apply for a job seeking permit at renewal, but you must prove that you are looking for another job or activity. You need to bring a registration certificate from the Employment Center

How long does this permit last?

  • The duration depends on the activity you carry out and the documents you submit, but usually it is 1 to 2 years.
  • It is renewable, if you continue to work independently and meet the required requirements, for 1-2 years.

When does the permit expire?

  • You must apply for renewal at least 60 days before expiry.
  • If you have the requirements, you can also convert it to another type of permit (study, family, EU long-term resident, employed work, etc.)

What rights does it give me / what can I access?

With this permit you can:

  • Work independently with VAT number
  • Work as an employee (employed work)
  • Obtain residence registration in the Municipality where you live
  • Register with the Health Service and have a general practitioner
  • Travel in the Schengen area with passport (for maximum 90 days every 180)
  • Apply for family reunification
  • Enroll in courses, schools, university

Can I renew it?

It can be renewed by filling out the postal kit, attaching the following documents:

  • Photocopy of the expiring residence permit
  • Photocopy of passport
  • Photocopy of VAT number assignment
  • Photocopy of registration with the Chamber of Commerce Industry Crafts
  • Photocopy of the authorization or license or registration in the appropriate register or of the submission of declaration or notification required by current regulations for the exercise of the professional activity carried out
  • Photocopy of tax return and provisional balance sheet

What if I'm unemployed at renewal time?

You can apply for the residence permit for job seeking, attaching your registration with the Employment Center!
