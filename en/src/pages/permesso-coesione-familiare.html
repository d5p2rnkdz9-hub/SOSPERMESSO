Family cohesion

Documents for first issuance Documents for renewal

What is this permit?

The residence permit for family cohesion is a type of reunification that you can apply for directly in Italy. It is for those who want to live together with their foreign family member who is already in Italy.

What is the difference from family reunification?

The family member applying for cohesion is already present in Italy and does not live abroad as in the case of family reunification, so it is not necessary to request the authorization for reunification from the Immigration Office of the Prefecture.

How long does this permit last?

It has the same duration as the residence permit of your family member already in Italy.

What are the requirements to obtain it?

To obtain this permit:

  • Your foreign family member must have a valid residence permit of at least one year or an EU permit for long-term residents
  • You must already be in Italy with a regular stay (even with a tourist visa still valid) or with a permit expired for no more than 12 months
  • You must prove the family relationship

You can apply for family cohesion with:

  • Your husband or wife at least 18 years old (only if you are not legally separated)
  • Your children under 18 years old (at the time of the application)
  • Your adult children who are dependent, if they cannot support themselves due to serious health problems
  • Your parents over 65 years old, if they have no other children in their country or if these cannot help them

Where do I apply for this permit?

You must submit the application via postal kit which you will then take to the post office. You can get help from a patronato or legal office to fill out the forms.

What can I do with this residence permit?

With this permit you can:

  • Get residence in the Municipality where you live
  • Work with a regular contract (self-employed or employed)
  • Register for free with the National Health Service (SSN) and have a family doctor
  • Travel in the Schengen area with your passport (for short stays up to 3 months)
  • Enroll in school, vocational training, or university

What happens when the permit expires?

You can renew it if you continue to have the requirements.

Can I convert this permit?

Yes, this permit can be converted to:

  • Work permit (self-employed or employed)
  • Job seeking permit
  • Study permit
