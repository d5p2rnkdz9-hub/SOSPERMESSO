Permit for Family (family reasons - art. 30) after entry with authorization

Documents for first issuance Documents for renewal

What is this residence permit?

It is the residence permit for family reasons that is given to family members who entered Italy with a visa for family reunification.

How do I request this permit?

You entered Italy with an entry visa, after your family member received the authorization for family reunification. Very good. Here are the next steps:

  • Within 8 days you must go to the Single Immigration Desk at the Prefecture of your territory.
  • The Single Immigration Desk will give you forms to fill out.
  • Go to the Post Office and send the forms.
  • The Post Office will give you an appointment at the Police Headquarters (Questura).
  • Go to the Police Headquarters (Questura) on the day of the appointment. They will take your fingerprints.
  • The Police Headquarters (Questura) will inform you when your permit is ready.

How long does it last?

The duration depends on the situation of the family member who requested the family reunification:

  • if the family member has refugee status or subsidiary protection, the residence permit for family reasons lasts 5 years.
  • if the family member has a long-term residence permit, the residence permit for family reasons lasts 2 years.
  • if the family member has a shorter residence permit (e.g. for work, study), the permit will have the same duration as the permit of the family member who requested the family reunification.

What rights does it give me / what do I have access to?

After requesting the permit, with the postal receipt you can:

  • Work with a regular contract
  • Register with the National Health Service (SSN) and have a general practitioner
  • Travel in Schengen countries for up to 90 days every 180 days
  • Register at the registry office and obtain residence

Can I renew it?

The renewal request must be made before the permit expires via postal kit.

At the time of the renewal request you must attach:

  • application form (available at the post office or the Police Headquarters (Questura))
  • copy of valid passport
  • copy of expiring residence permit
  • tax code
  • family status and residence certificate
  • residence permit of the family member who requested the family reunification (if foreign)
  • hospitality declaration or rental/property contract
  • marriage or kinship certificate (if applicable)
  • proof of means of support (if required, such as pay slip or tax return).
  • receipt of postal payment slip

Can I convert it?

Before expiration, you can convert your permit to:

  • work permit (if you have an employment contract)
  • study permit (if you are enrolled in a course)
  • job-seeking permit (if you worked before and are looking for a new job)
