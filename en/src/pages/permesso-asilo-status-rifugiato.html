Permit for Asylum (refugee status)

Documents for first issuance Documents for renewal

What is this permit?

This is the permit given to those who flee their country because they risk persecution for their religion, political opinion, nationality, or because they belong to a certain ethnic or social group.

How do I apply for this permit?

To get this permit you must:

  • Apply for international protection (see our guide)
  • Obtain a positive decision from the Territorial Commission or the Court
  • With this positive decision, go to the Police Headquarters (Questura) to apply for the permit

Where do I apply for this permit?

You must go in person to the Police Headquarters (Questura).

What documents do I need to apply?

To apply for the permit you need to bring the decision from the Commission (or Court) that recognized you as a refugee

How long does it last?

The permit lasts 5 years and can be renewed, usually automatically, for another 5 years

What rights does it give me? What do I have access to?

With this permit you can:

  • Register your residence in your municipality
  • Work legally (employed or self-employed)
  • Register with the National Health Service (SSN) and have a family doctor
  • Enroll in school, vocational training, or university
  • Receive financial and housing assistance
  • Apply for Italian citizenship after 5 years of legal residence (counted from the date you applied for protection)
  • Apply for family reunification with close relatives:
    • wife/husband
    • minor children
    • adult children if totally disabled
    • parents over 65 or dependent

For family reunification you do NOT have to prove you have a large house and minimum income like other foreigners.

Can I travel outside Italy?

You can travel freely in Italy, go to other EU countries for up to 3 months and, with the "travel document" (a passport given to refugees), also to other countries in the world.

WARNING! You cannot travel to your country of origin. If you do, the Police Headquarters (Questura) could revoke your residence permit.

Can I convert this permit?

The only permit it makes sense to convert the asylum permit to is the EU residence permit for long-term residents ("Carta"), after five years of residence in Italy.

It is not possible to convert the political asylum permit into a residence permit for self-employed or employed work.
