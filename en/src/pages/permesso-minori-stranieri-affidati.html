Permit for entrusted foreign minors

Documents for first issuance Documents for renewal

What is it?

It is the residence permit for family reasons that is given to foreign minors who arrived in Italy without their parents (MSNA) who have been entrusted to a relative within the fourth degree (brother/sister, uncle/aunt, cousin, grandfather/grandmother...).

If you don't have relatives in Italy, you will be given a residence permit for unaccompanied foreign minor (MSNA).

The family reasons permit is better because when you turn 18 it will be easier to convert to another type of permit.

How long does it last?

It is valid until you turn 18 years old.

What are the conditions to obtain it?

You can obtain this permit if:

  • You have been entrusted to a relative within the fourth degree (brother/sister, uncle/aunt, cousin, grandfather/grandmother...) with a document from the Juvenile Court

Where do you apply for this permit?

You must apply directly at the Police Headquarters (Questura).

What can I do with this permit?

With this permit you can:

  • Enroll in school, vocational training, or university
  • Enroll in the National Health Service (SSN) and have a general practitioner
  • Work with a regular contract (self-employed or employed), but only after age 16

And when I turn 18?

Before turning 18, you can change your permit to:

  • Study permit (if you are enrolled in school or university)
  • Work permit (if you have a self-employed or employed work contract)
  • Job seeking permit (if you are looking for work)
