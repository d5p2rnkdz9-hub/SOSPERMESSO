Permit for Minor assistance (Art. 31)

Documents for first issuance Documents for renewal

What is this permit?

The residence permit for minor assistance is given when the Juvenile Court allows the entry or stay in Italy of a relative of a foreign minor for serious reasons related to the child's growth and well-being.

How long does this permit last?

The duration is established by the Juvenile Court and is normally at least one year. The permit is renewable.

What are the requirements to obtain it?

The fundamental requirements for this permit are:

  • The minor must be present in Italian territory
  • The applicant must be a relative of the minor (usually a parent)
  • It is necessary to demonstrate that without the relative the minor would suffer greatly, both emotionally and physically, especially if the child is young
  • The relative must provide documentation showing the family relationship with the minor
  • Documents proving your children's journey in Italy (school, activities, etc.).

Where do I apply for this permit?

The application must be made to the Juvenile Court of the area where the minor lives. Having a lawyer is not mandatory: parents can submit the appeal independently.

WARNING: The court case can take months or even years and the outcome is uncertain. While waiting for the Court's decision you will not have a provisional residence permit.

After obtaining the Court document, relatives must apply for the residence permit at the Police Headquarters (Questura) of the area where they live. The permit is issued in electronic format, without need to send the postal kit to the Italian Post Office.

What documents do I need to apply?

When applying to the Juvenile Court, you will need to bring:

  • Appeal application (a template is available)
  • Revenue stamp of 27.00 EUR
  • Birth certificate of the minor
  • Identity documents of the applicant relative and the minor
  • Any medical certificates, if they attest to health problems of the minor

What can I do with this residence permit?

With this permit you can:

  • Work with a regular contract (self-employed or employed)
  • Register with the National Health Service (SSN) and have healthcare: have a family doctor
  • Enroll in school, vocational training, or university
  • Travel freely in Italy and Schengen countries (for short stays up to 3 months) with passport

What happens when the permit expires?

You can renew it or convert it to another permit

WARNING: To renew this permit you must start a new case at the Juvenile Court. It cannot be renewed at the Police Headquarters (Questura) or with the postal kit.

For conversion, this permit can be changed to:

  • Work residence permit (self-employed or employed)
  • EU permit for long-term residents after 5 years of regular stay in Italy
