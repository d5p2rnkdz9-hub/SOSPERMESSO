Family reunification is the procedure that allows a foreigner legally residing in Italy to bring certain close family members from abroad.

The reunited family member receives a residence permit for family reasons.

ğŸ“‹ Complete procedure

â±ï¸ Total duration: approximately 8-12 months

PHASE 1: Application for clearance certificate (nulla osta)

Where: Single Immigration Desk at the Prefecture

Who: The applicant (person living in Italy)

Documents:

  • Completed application form
  • 16 euro revenue stamp
  • Copy of valid residence permit
  • Copy of passport
  • Housing suitability certificate
  • Tax return (CU/UNICO)
  • Documents proving family relationship:
    • Marriage certificate (for spouse)
    • Birth certificate (for children)
    • Translated, legalized, apostilled
  • Health insurance

PHASE 2: Waiting for clearance certificate (nulla osta)

Timing: 180 days (6 months) for response

The Single Desk verifies the requirements and issues the clearance certificate.

PHASE 3: Visa application

Where: Italian Embassy/Consulate in the country of origin

Who: The family member to be reunited

When: Within 6 months of clearance certificate issuance

Documents:

  • Original clearance certificate
  • Valid passport
  • Passport photo
  • Family certificates

PHASE 4: Entry into Italy

The family member enters Italy with the entry visa.

PHASE 5: Residence permit

Where: Police Headquarters (Questura)

When: Within 8 days of entry

The family member receives the permit for family reasons.

â±ï¸ Duration of reunited family member's permit

First issuance: Same duration as applicant's permit (max 2 years)

Renewal: 2 years

Characteristics:

  • Allows working (employed and self-employed)
  • Allows studying
  • Renewable independently (without depending on applicant)
