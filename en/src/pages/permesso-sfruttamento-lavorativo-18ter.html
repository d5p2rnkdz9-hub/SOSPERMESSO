Permit for Labor exploitation (18ter)

Documents for first issuance Documents for renewal

üîπ Art. 18-ter TUI (labor exploitation - gangmastering)

  • For victims of the crime under art. 603-bis c.p.
  • Issued by the Police Chief (Questore) upon:
    • initiative of the Public Prosecutor
    • or opinion of the Labor Inspectorate
  • Duration: 12 months, renewable (even longer for justice reasons).
  • Also valid for family members (permit for family reasons).

3) Is a formal complaint required?

No.

A formal complaint is not mandatory.

However, it is required:

  • that exploitation emerges in investigations / inspections / criminal proceedings
  • useful cooperation from the victim to clarify the facts and identify those responsible.

4) Do undocumented persons risk deportation?

No.

After applying for the permit under art. 18-ter:

  • the judge declares no grounds for prosecution for the crime of illegal stay (art. 10-bis TUI).

5) Can you work while waiting for the permit?

Yes.

With the receipt from the Police Headquarters (Questura) you can:

  • stay legally
  • work
  • register with the SSN (National Health Service)
  • access social services and employment centers
  • apply for INPS benefits (ADI, universal allowance, etc.)

6) Rights with the 18-ter permit

It allows:

  • working (employed or self-employed)
  • studying
  • registering with the civil registry
  • accessing welfare services
  • entering the SAI (reception system)
  • registering with SIISL (INPS employment platform)

7) Renewal and conversion

  • Renewal: for 1 year or more if needed for justice reasons.
  • Conversion possible to:
    • work
    • study
    • job seeking

8) Assistance measures (art. 6 DL 145/24)

Personalized programs for:

  • housing
  • health
  • training
  • job placement

Also valid for family members.

9) When can the permit be revoked

For example if:

  • the conditions of exploitation cease
  • the person commits serious crimes
  • renounces the assistance measures
  • conduct incompatible with the purposes of the permit

10) Other important rights

  • State-funded legal aid (even without income limits).
  • Inclusion allowance even without 5 years of residence.
  • Universal allowance (if there are children).
