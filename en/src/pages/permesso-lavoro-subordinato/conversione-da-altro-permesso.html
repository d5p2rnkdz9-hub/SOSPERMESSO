Employed work following conversion from another permit

This page contains only the specific information for conversion from another permit.

For general information (what it is, duration, rights), see the main page.

What is this permit?

The residence permit for employed work is given to foreigners whowork as employees of an employer with a regular contract. This means you have a boss who pays you a salary and you are their employee.

How do I apply for this permit?

The residence permit for employed work can be issued in two situations: -entering Italy with a clearance certificate or visa for employed work, with the quotas provided by the annual immigration quota decree(1)-converting a previous residence permit to employed work(2)

‚úàÔ∏è1: you entered Italy with a clearance certificate and visa for employed work:

  1. within8 daysof your arrival in Italy go with your employer to theSingle Immigration Deskat the Prefecture in the area where you work
  2. The Single Desk will have you sign the employment contract and give you the forms to fill out
  3. Go to thepost officeand send the forms to the Police Headquarters (Questura)
  4. The Post Office will give you an appointment at the Police Headquarters (Questura)
  5. Go to the Police Headquarters (Questura) on the appointment day for fingerprinting
  6. The Police Headquarters (Questura) will tell you when your permit is ready

What documents do I need to apply?

  • Valid passport
  • Entry visafor employed work
  • Signed employment contract
  • Copy of your employer'sidentity document(if Italian or European) or copy of their residence permit (if foreign)
  • 4 recent passport photos
  • Revenue stampof 16.00 euros
  • Payment by postal money orderof 80.46 euros

üîÑ 2: you had another residence permit, then you applied via postal kit for conversion to employed work permit

In this case, the Post Office gave you an appointment at the Police Headquarters (Questura)

  1. Go to the Police Headquarters (Questura) on the appointment day for fingerprinting
  2. If everything is in order, the Police Headquarters (Questura) will tell you when your permit is ready

What documents do I need to apply?

  • Valid passport
  • Signed employment contract
  • Copy of your employer'sidentity document(if Italian or European) or copy of their residence permit (if foreign)
  • 4 recent passport photos
  • Revenue stampof 16.00 euros
  • Payment by postal money orderof 80.46 euros

What are the conditions to obtain it?

To get this permit you must have aregular employment contractwith an Italian employer and apayslipof at least 538 euros per month, approximately.

How long does this permit last?

The duration depends on the type of employment contract you have. Usually:

  • 2 yearsif your employment contract is fixed-term (has an end date)
  • 3 yearsif your employment contract is permanent (has no end date)

What rights does it give me? What can I access?

With this permit you can:

  • Workwith a regular contract (employed or self-employed)
  • Register your residencein the Municipality where you live
  • Enroll in the National Health Service (SSN)and have a general practitioner
  • Travel in the Schengen areawith passport (for stays up to 3 months).
  • Enroll in school, vocational training, or university
  • Apply for family reunification

!! You cannot work in another European country

What happens if I lose my job?

If you lose your jobyou don't lose your permituntil it expires. You can:

  • Look for a new job
  • Register on unemployment lists
  • Apply for apermit forjob seekingat renewal

And when the permit expires? Can I renew it? Can I convert it?

Before it expires you have two options:

  1. Renew it:renewal must be requested 60 days before the expiration date, via postal kit.

What documents are needed?

  • Old residence permit
  • Valid passport
  • Current employment contract
  • Copy of employer's document
  • Payslips or tax returnfrom the previous year
  • Certificate of residence or hospitality declaration
  • 16 euro revenue stamp
  • Payment by postal money orderof 80.46 euros

2. Convert it:if you have all the necessary requirements, this permit can be converted to a residence permit for:

  • Study
  • Job seeking
  • Family reasons
  • EU permit for long-term residents (if you have at least 5 years of residence)

What is this permit?

It is a residence permit for those whowork independently, meaningthey don't have an employer, but carry out a work or professional activity on their own (for example: merchant, craftsman, freelance professional, etc.).

How do I apply for this permit?

!! There are two different cases:

  1. ‚úàÔ∏èYou entered Italy with a visa for self-employment
  2. üîÑYou want to convert another type of permit to a self-employment permit

1. ‚úàÔ∏è You entered Italy with a visa for self-employment

What you need to do:

  1. You must have already obtained avisa for self-employmentfrom the Italian consulate in your country.
  2. Within8 days of your arrival in Italy, fill outForm 1 and Form 2(postal kit) and send it from anauthorized post office.
  3. You will receive an appointment at thePolice Headquarters (Questura)for fingerprinting.
  4. The Police Headquarters (Questura) will tell you when to pick up your permit.

Documents to attach:

  • Form 1 and 2from the kit, filled out and signed
  • Photocopyof your entire passport
  • Photocopy of thecertificationfrom the Italian diplomatic/consular representation in your country, confirming that you have therequirements for the self-employment visa(as per art. 26 D.Lgs. 286/98 and art. 39 DPR 394/99)
  • 4 passport photos
  • Revenue stampof 16.00 euros
  • Payment bypostal money order of 80.46 euros

2. üîÑ You want to convert another type of permit to a self-employment permit

Example: you have a permit for study, family, job seeking, etc., and now you want to work as a self-employed worker.

What you need to do:

  1. Fill out thepostal kit(Form 1 and 2) and send it from an authorized post office.

    Documents to attach:

  2. You will receive an appointment at the Police Headquarters (Questura) for fingerprinting.
  3. If the Police Headquarters (Questura) accepts the conversion, you will receive the new self-employment permit.

What happens if you close your business?

  • You can apply for ajob seeking permitat renewal, but you must prove you are looking for another job or business. You need to bring a certificate of registration at the Employment Center

How long does this permit last?

  • The duration depends on the activity you perform and the documents you present, but usually it is1 to 2 years.
  • It isrenewable, if you continue to work independently and meet the required requirements, for 1-2 years.

When does the permit expire?

  • You mustrequest renewalat least60 days before expiration.
  • Ifyou have the requirements, you can alsoconvert itto another type of permit (study, family, long-term residents, employed work, etc.)

What rights does it give me / what can I access?

With this permit you can:

  • Work independentlywith VAT number
  • Work as an employee (employed work)
  • Register your residencein the Municipality where you live
  • Enroll in the Health Serviceand have a general practitioner
  • Travel in the Schengen areawith passport (for maximum 90 days every 180)
  • Apply for family reunification
  • Enroll in courses, schools, universities

Can I renew it?

It can be renewed by filling out the postal kit, attaching the following documents:

  • Photocopy of the expiring residence permit
  • Photocopy of passport
  • Photocopy of VAT number attribution
  • Photocopy of registration with the Chamber of Commerce, Industry and Crafts
  • Photocopy of authorization or license or registration in the appropriate register or presentation of declaration or report required by current regulations for the professional activity performed
  • Photocopy of tax return and provisional balance sheet

If at renewal time I am unemployed?

You can apply for thejob seeking residence permit, attaching your Employment Center registration!
